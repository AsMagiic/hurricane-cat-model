## Part A — Frequency: distributional validation

### A1. Marginal Poisson dispersion test

> **Scope:** marginal (constant-rate) Poisson over the satellite era (1966–2024, N=59 years). The production model is a Poisson GLM conditioned on AMO; marginal over-dispersion is *expected* as the signature of active/quiet-season clustering — the variance the AMO covariate absorbs. Residual dispersion of the fitted GLM (the operative diagnostic for production mis-specification) is not computed here — it requires re-fitting the GLM with downloaded climate indices.

| Statistic | Value |
|---|---|
| Satellite-era window | 1966–2024 |
| N (years) | 59 |
| Mean annual count | 0.475 events/yr |
| Variance | 0.702 |
| Index of Dispersion (IoD = var/mean) | 1.479 |
| Chi² statistic D = (N−1)×IoD | 85.79 (df=58) |
| p-value (chi²) | 0.0103 |

**Finding:** Marginal counts over-dispersed (IoD=1.48, p=0.0103). Expected signature of AMO-driven active/quiet-season clustering — the covariate the production GLM conditions on. Residual dispersion of the fitted GLM not tested here. NB-GLM motivated as future work.

**Caveat:** Chi-squared p-value is approximate at this low mean (lambda~0.47 events/yr). Over-dispersion is corroborated by visible active-season clustering (e.g., 3 landfalls in 1985 and 1992).

### A2. Rate consistency: production lambda vs observed 2006–2023

> Production lambda is an AMO-conditioned GLM rate evaluated at the 2013–2022 mean climate. This window (2006–2023) aligns with the covariate-available period. Physical context: the documented 2006–2016 FL major-hurricane drought (basin active / FL quiet) depresses FL-specific counts below what a basin-scale AMO predictor would expect.

| Statistic | Value |
|---|---|
| Production lambda | 0.6576 events/yr |
| Window length | 18 years |
| Expected count (lambda × years) | 11.84 |
| Observed count | 6 |
| P(X ≤ observed) under Poisson(expected) | 0.0502 |
| 95% Poisson CI | [6, 19] |

**Finding:** Observed count inside 95% Poisson CI — rate consistent

## Part B — Intensity: out-of-sample test

> **Train:** FL HU landfalls 1851–2000 (n=97). Full pre-2001 record used; pre-satellite Vmax bias is non-directional (measurement error, not count-detection bias). **Test:** 2001–2024 (n=15). At n=15, the test is power-limited for small effects — but a rejection is correspondingly robust.

### B1. Train-period fit

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Distribution | Truncated lognormal (lower bound 64 kt) |
| mu (log-kt) | 4.4211 |
| sigma (dimensionless) | 0.2406 |
| Fitted median | 86.7 kt |
| Fitted Cat4+ fraction | 11.8% |

### B2. KS test (2001–2024, n=15)

| Statistic | Value |
|---|---|
| KS D statistic | 0.4620 |
| p-value | 0.0019 |

**Finding:** Rejects at n=15 (p=0.0019); robust finding — with n=15, low power makes rejection hard, so the rejection confirms a large effect.

### B3. Descriptive comparison

| Metric | Fitted (train) | Observed (test) |
|---|---|---|
| Median (kt) | 86.7 | 105.4 |
| Cat4+ fraction | 11.8% | 33.3% (5/15) |

**Attribution:** The KS rejection and elevated Cat4+ fraction (33% observed vs 12% expected) reflect post-2000 intensification AND/OR evolving intensity-estimation methods (modern aircraft recon and SFMR capture peak winds that pre-satellite-era methods systematically under-estimated). The two effects cannot be separated from HURDAT2 Vmax alone; the model's stationary-climate intensity distribution is a declared limitation.
